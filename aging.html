Manure Aging Guide

  1. What is Manure Aging?
    • The process of allowing animal manure to decompose and stabilize over time before it is applied to soil.
  2. Benefits.
    • Reduces pathogens and weed seeds, making it safer for plants and soil.
    • Improves nutrient availability for crops.
    • Enhances soil structure and moisture retention.
  3. Application
    • Use aged manure as a soil amendment or fertilizer to boost soil health.

Procedure for Aging Manure

Step 1: Collect Fresh Manure

Gather fresh manure from livestock, ensuring it is free from contaminants and weeds.

Step 2: Choose a Location

Select a shaded area away from water sources to prevent runoff and contamination.

Step 3: Create a Manure Pile

Build a pile using layers of manure, allowing for airflow to aid decomposition.

Step 4: Monitor Temperature

Check the temperature of the pile; it should reach 130-150Â°F (54-65Â°C) to kill pathogens.

Step 5: Turn the Pile

Turn the manure pile every few weeks to promote even decomposition and reduce odor.

Step 6: Allow to Age

Let the manure age for 6 months to a year to ensure it is safe for plants and reduces pathogens.

Organic fertilizers guide

  1. What is Organic Fertilizer?
    • Fertilizer made from natural material and plant residue.
  2. Benefits.
    • Improve soil fertility, and structure.
    • Environmentally friendly.
  3. Application
    • Spread arround the soil and mix it.

Procedure for Making Organic Fertilizer

Step 1: Collect Organic Waste

Gather vegetable scraps, fruit peels, grass clippings, leaves, and other organic waste materials.

Step 2: Shred Large Materials

Shred larger organic materials into smaller pieces to speed up the decomposition process.

Step 3: Create a Compost Pile

Start layering green (nitrogen-rich) and brown (carbon-rich) materials to build your compost pile.

Step 4: Add Water

Maintain moisture by adding water, but be careful not to overwater. Keep it damp, not soaking wet.

Step 5: Turn the Pile

Turn the compost pile every few days to aerate it and speed up the decomposition process.

Step 6: Monitor and Use

When the compost turns dark and crumbly, it's ready to use as organic fertilizer for your plants.
